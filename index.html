Towards Superhuman Motion Prediction
Workshop (proposed) ICRA 2019

ICRA 2019, Montreal, Canada

Objectives

Motion prediction is a key technology for autonomous systems working in dynamic and crowded environments, where unforeseen humans' movements may undermine the system operation and cause harm to the humans' lives. Accurate motion predictions enable autonomous systems to plan safer and comfortable actions and to efficiently accomplish the desired tasks. Several successful methods have been developed in the recent years, by leveraging the potential of machine learning, game theory and probabilist planning techniques, together with the use of attributes and semantic knowledge from the environments and agents moving in the scene. The aim of this workshop is to discuss on the recent developments on motion prediction techniques, their current limitations,benchmarking and new avenues to improve up to super-human levels.

Topics of interest

Topics of interest for this workshop include:

  • Human motion prediction
  • Human activity recognition and forecasting
  • Learning usual human motion behaviors
  • Motion and Task Planning in dynamic environments considering motion predictions
  • Benchmarking of prediction algorithms

Intended audience

The topic of human motion prediction is of interest for researchers from different scientific areas, such as motion planning, learning and control, human-robot interaction, intelligent transportation systems and computer vision. However, it is rarely in the spotlight and is usually treated as small and single component part of a larger research problem. This workshop aims to build a platform for researchers interested in the development of reliable motion prediction approaches and tools to evaluate their performance and quality. It will feature a diverse set of high-profile invited speakers both form academia and industry. Their expertise is covering a wide spectrum of topics, including computer vision, human-robot interaction, autonomous vehicles.
